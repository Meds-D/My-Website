I am currently pursuing an undergraduate degree at Ashoka University, majoring in Economics with a minor in Mathematics. I am deeply interested in research, and my areas of interest range from Development Economics, specifically gender-related issues, to more macroeconomic topics such as trade and firm dynamics.

I believe academia requires a fair bit of creativity. I often seek innovative ways to test economic hypotheses, whether by leveraging limited data to draw meaningful insights or devising theoretical models. This creativity also extends to more conventional artistic pursuits—my non-academic interests include filmmaking and painting.

I have made a couple of short films and documentaries, often documenting my travels. I have been painting my whole life, receiving professional training and later continuing as a self-taught artist. You can find more details about my artistic pursuits in the Interests section.
